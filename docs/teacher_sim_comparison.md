# Chalk & Chance Teacher-Simulator Comparison

## Why this game can be outstanding

Most teacher simulators optimize one of four strengths:

- Mixed-reality avatar practice: Mursion / TeachLivE emphasizes live rehearsal with avatars, authenticity, confidence, and reduced risk to real students.
- Dynamic simulation and reporting: simSchool emphasizes AI-informed virtual students, repeated decision practice, and personalized feedback.
- Text scenario reflection: Teacher Moments emphasizes low-cost approximations of hard conversations and structured reflection.
- Emerging AI classroom agents: recent systems such as ArguMath and SimClass emphasize AI-generated students, discussion orchestration, and feedback.

Chalk & Chance should not compete by imitating any one of these. Its edge is a playable, evidence-instrumented teaching rehearsal:

- It is a game loop, not only a role-play session. Badges, XP, items, mission unlocks, capstones, and replay pressure make deliberate practice feel like progression.
- It scores whole-class orchestration, not only the quality of a single dialogue. Attention drift, proximity, facing direction, interruptions, equity, and group pods are first-class mechanics.
- It makes differentiated student needs mechanically real. Each persona has a target need and move profile, so the same "good teacher move" does not work on everyone.
- It leaves construct-level evidence. Turns log move tags, wait time, target-fit judgments, xAPI-style statements, and live ECD competency estimates.
- It closes each mode with a readable debrief. Encounters, lectures, gym capstones, group check-ins, and full lessons all expose score drivers, next focus, and reward/progression state instead of disappearing after a timer.
- It can become a research artifact and a playable training product at the same time.

## Comparison Table

| Reference point | Typical strength | Common gap | Chalk & Chance response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mursion / TeachLivE | Authentic avatar rehearsal and human-like interaction | Cost, scheduling, human-in-loop dependence, limited repeatable telemetry | Offline-first Godot loop, replayable missions, construct telemetry |
| simSchool | Dynamic students and reporting | Less game-like moment-to-moment classroom feel | Pixel RPG loop, badges, items, encounter feedback, classroom ecology |
| Teacher Moments | Low-cost scenario prompts and reflection | Narrower conversational situations | Full lesson flow: lecture, discussion, group work, independent work, capstone |
| ArguMath / SimClass-style AI classroom sims | AI agents and discourse feedback | Often framed as system demonstration rather than game progression | Evidence-grounded mechanics plus progression, UI, telemetry, and playability |

## Product Signature

Position Chalk & Chance as:

> A cozy RPG-style teacher rehearsal game where every teaching move changes the classroom ecology and every practice session leaves research-grade evidence.

The in-game signature should be visible in three places:

- Mission hub: show the research signature of each mission before play.
- Lesson preview: explain what construct evidence the custom scenario can produce.
- Debrief: summarize the evidence fingerprint, including attention, equity, wait time, target-fit moves, and takeover attempts.
